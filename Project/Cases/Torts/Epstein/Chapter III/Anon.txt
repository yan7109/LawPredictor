Synopsis of Rule of Law
If a statute gives a person a right, it also gives that person the right to receive remedy from anyone who violates his right by that statute.

Facts
Facts not revealed in the casebook.

Issue
There was no ascertainable issue in the casebook.

Held
If a statute gives a person a right, it also gives that person the right to receive remedy from anyone who violates his right by that statute.

Discussion
If the government enacts a statute, then those wronged by people violating that statute have a remedy in law, not merely in equity.
